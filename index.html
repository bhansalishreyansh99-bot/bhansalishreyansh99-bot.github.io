Knowledge Base

FREQUENTLY
ASKED QUESTIONS

Everything you need to know about our practice areas, your obligations, and how we can help.

DPDP Compliance
What is the DPDP Act?+

The Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 is India's comprehensive data protection law that governs how businesses collect, process, store, and share digital personal data. It came with the DPDP Rules, 2025 which lay out the specific compliance requirements.

Does my business need to comply with the DPDP Act?+

If your business collects or processes digital personal data of individuals in India, whether you're based in India or abroad, the DPDP Act applies to you. This includes websites, apps, SaaS platforms, e-commerce stores, and any digital service that handles user data.

What are the penalties for non-compliance?+

Penalties under the DPDP Act can go up to ₹250 crore depending on the nature and severity of the breach. Even failing to notify the Data Protection Board of a data breach within 72 hours can attract significant penalties.

Do you help with data breach crisis communications?+

Yes. Under the DPDP Act, you're required to notify affected individuals and the Data Protection Board within specific timelines. We help draft compliant notifications, manage public communications, and coordinate your legal response to minimise reputational and regulatory damage.

Global Privacy Compliance
I already comply with GDPR. Do I still need separate DPDP compliance?+

Yes. While GDPR and DPDP share some principles, they have distinct requirements around consent, data breach notification timelines, children's data, and penalties. A GDPR compliant framework is a good starting point, but it won't fully satisfy DPDP obligations.

Which privacy laws apply to my business if I operate internationally?+

It depends on where your users are located and where you process their data. Common frameworks include GDPR in the EU, CCPA and CPRA in California, PDPA in Singapore and Thailand, and DPDP in India. We map your data flows and identify exactly which laws apply.

Can you build one compliance framework that covers multiple jurisdictions?+

Yes, that's exactly what we specialise in. We design unified privacy frameworks that satisfy multiple regulations simultaneously, so you don't need separate compliance programs for each country.

Software & SaaS Contracts
Why do I need a lawyer for SaaS contracts?+

Standard templates don't account for your specific IP ownership, liability caps, data handling obligations, or exit terms. A poorly drafted SaaS agreement can expose you to unlimited liability, IP disputes, or data breaches with no recourse.

What should a good SaaS agreement cover?+

At minimum it should cover service scope and SLAs, data ownership and processing terms, IP assignment or licensing, limitation of liability, indemnification, termination and data portability, confidentiality, and dispute resolution.

Do you draft contracts for both SaaS providers and customers?+

Yes. We work on both sides, helping SaaS companies draft their customer facing agreements, and helping businesses review and negotiate vendor SaaS contracts before signing.

Intellectual Property Management
When should I register my trademark?+

As early as possible, ideally before you launch publicly. In India, trademark rights are largely first to file, so delaying registration risks someone else filing your brand name. The process takes 8 to 12 months but protection begins from the filing date.

What's the difference between a trademark, copyright, and patent?+

A trademark protects brand identifiers like names, logos, and slogans. Copyright protects original creative works such as code, content, and designs. Patents protect inventions and novel processes. Most tech businesses need a combination of all three.

Can you help with IP disputes and enforcement?+

Yes. We handle trademark oppositions, takedown notices for copyright infringement, cease and desist actions, and coordinate with enforcement authorities when needed. We also assist with IP portfolio audits to identify unprotected assets.

Cyber Insurance Advisory
What does cyber insurance cover?+

Typical policies cover data breach response costs, business interruption losses, ransomware payments, legal defence costs, regulatory fines where insurable, and third party claims arising from a cyber incident. But coverage varies significantly between policies.

Do I really need cyber insurance?+

If your business handles any digital personal data, processes online payments, or relies on digital infrastructure then yes. A single data breach can cost lakhs to crores in response costs, legal fees, and regulatory penalties. Cyber insurance transfers that financial risk.

How do you help with cyber insurance?+

We review policy language to identify gaps and exclusions, map your actual risk exposure against coverage, help you negotiate better terms with insurers, and provide claims support if an incident occurs. We translate insurance jargon into plain language so you know exactly what you're buying.

Cyber Jurisdiction
What is cyber jurisdiction?+

Cyber jurisdiction refers to determining which country's or state's laws apply when a digital incident crosses borders, such as a data breach involving servers in one country, users in another, and the company headquartered in a third.

My servers are outside India. Can Indian law still apply to me?+

Yes. Under the DPDP Act, if you process personal data in connection with offering goods or services to individuals in India, Indian law applies regardless of where your servers are located.

What happens if there's a conflict between two countries' data laws?+

This is exactly where cyber jurisdiction expertise matters. We analyse applicable laws, identify conflicts, advise on which obligations take priority, and structure your operations to minimise jurisdictional risk. In some cases, data localisation or contractual structuring can resolve conflicts.

Reputation Management
What can you do about defamatory content online?+

We issue legal takedown notices to platforms, send cease and desist letters to publishers, file court applications for removal orders where needed, and coordinate with intermediaries under the IT Act to get harmful content removed as quickly as possible.

How quickly can defamatory content be removed?+

It depends on the platform and severity. Social media platforms typically respond within 24 to 72 hours for clear violations. Court ordered removals take longer but carry stronger enforcement. We prioritise the fastest available route for each situation.

Can you help protect my personal privacy and online identity?+

Absolutely. Whether it's unauthorised use of your photos, personal information being circulated without consent, doxxing, impersonation accounts, or private data appearing in search results, we help individuals take legal action to reclaim control over their digital identity. This includes right to erasure requests, platform reporting, legal notices, and where necessary, court interventions to ensure your personal privacy is respected and restored.
